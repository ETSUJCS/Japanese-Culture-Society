About Us:

The Japanese Culture Society is a student-led organization at ETSU dedicated to exploring and celebrating Japanese culture. Our mission is to foster an appreciation for Japan’s rich traditions, modern influences, and language through engaging activities and events.

For more information about ETSU's Literature and Language department, check out the ETSU Literature and Language Home Page.

What We Do:

We have weekly meetings on Wednesdays at 6:30PM in Sam Wilson Hall Room 322. Those meetings will be held on one of these topics:

  • Cultural Events – Experience traditional and modern Japanese festivities, including seasonal celebrations and cultural showcases.
  • Language & Learning – Participate in Japanese language practice sessions and discussions about Japan’s history, customs, and etiquette.
  • Food & Cooking – Learn about and try Japanese cuisine through cooking events and food tastings.
  • Community & Networking – Meet fellow enthusiasts, collaborate on fun projects, and make lasting friendships with those who share an interest in Japanese culture.

Membership:

Throughout the semester, we host a variety of events that allow members and guests to experience Japanese culture firsthand. From hands-on food-making workshops to cultural celebrations and guest speaker sessions, our events provide a unique opportunity to learn, connect, and immerse yourself in Japan’s rich traditions.

To help fund these experiences, we offer club memberships. While many events are open to everyone, some require an admission fee for non-members. By becoming a member, you gain free access to all paid events, making it a cost-effective way to participate while supporting the club. Additionally, members have the option to purchase an exclusive Japanese Culture Society T-shirt with their membership.

Membership Pricing:

  • $20 – Includes a club membership and a Japanese Culture Society T-shirt for the Fall 2024 & Spring 2025 semesters.
  • $15 – Membership only (no T-shirt) for both semesters.
  • Non-members can still attend paid events by paying an admission fee of $2–$5, depending on the event.

Members will also receive priority access to events if materials or food supplies are limited.

Some of our paid events include:

  • Mochi Making – Learn how to prepare and enjoy this beloved Japanese rice cake.
  • Sushi Making – Discover the art of crafting sushi and nigiri.
  • Snack Tastings – Sample a variety of traditional and modern Japanese snacks.
  • Guest Speaker Sessions – Engage with experts on Japanese language, culture, and history.
